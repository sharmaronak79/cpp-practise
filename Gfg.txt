
C++ Identifiers
All C++ variables must be identified 
with unique names.

These unique names are called 
identifiers.

Identifiers can be short names 
(like x and y) or more descriptive 
names (age, sum, totalVolume).

The general rules for constructing 
names for variables (unique 
identifiers) are:

Names can contain letters, digits 
and underscores
Names must begin with a letter 
or an underscore (_)
Names are case sensitive 
(myVar and myvar are different 
variables)
Names cannot contain whitespaces 
or special characters like !, #, %,
 etc.
